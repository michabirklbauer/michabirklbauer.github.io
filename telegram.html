The perfect messenger doesn't exist...

Posted by Micha Birklbauer on August 23, 2019

Telegram

Whatsapp, Telegram, Threema, Signal, Wire... the list of (more or less) secure instant messengers steadily grows and the quest of finding the "perfect" messenger keeps becoming a more and more difficult task. At least or especially then if you are not one of those sheeple who are stuck on Whatsapp and think it is a good and secure choice now that it is end-to-end encrypted (seriously it is not). But worry no more because I actually got the answer to all your questions: Telegram.

Telegram recently celebrated their 6th birthday and I have to admit, my account is probably almost as old. Back when Telegram launched it was one of the first serious alternatives to Whatsapp and it didn't take much to convince me to make the change. Even though I tried a lot of alternatives over the years like Threema and Signal, none of them ever came close to my user experience with Telegram. To me Telegram is the perfect trade-off between feature richness and security while still being able to offer their services for free. I don't want to go into too much details about features and security because everyone who is interested can just head over to https://telegram.org/faq and read all about it or simply try their app https://telegram.org/. BUT there is one more thing I wanted to point out: As a developer (and user) Telegram is simply a dream come true. Their APIs are well documented with good examples that are easy enough even for web newbies like me to understand which ultimately just makes their products really convenient and enjoyable to use. Following up on that I am proudly announcing that there is now a Telegram comment section on my main page and there will probably be more when new posts arrive. Last but not least all of my new posts will now feature Telegram's Instant View for more convenient reading within Telegram.
