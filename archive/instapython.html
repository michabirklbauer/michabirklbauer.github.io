Deprecation of the public Instagram API

And what it means for my projects

Posted by Micha Birklbauer on December 10, 2018

Introduction

To quote Instagram's developer website:

To continuously improve Instagram users' privacy and security, we are accelerating the deprecation of Instagram API Platform, making the following changes effective immediately. We understand that this may affect your business or services, and we appreciate your support in keeping our platform secure.

These capabilities will be disabled immediately (previously set for July 31, 2018 or December 11, 2018 deprecation). The following will be deprecated according to the timeline we shared previously:
  • Public Content - all remaining capabilities to read public media on a user's behalf on December 11, 2018
  • Basic - to read a userâ€™s own profile info and media in early 2020
For your reference, information on the new Instagram Graph API.
Long story short: Instagram deprecated and closed their public API which was openly accessible until today. Unfortunately most of my projects dealing with Instagram are dependent on this public API.

The future of my Instagram projects

GUI of InstaPython-App, Picture (c) Hanni Bodocian

So here come the bad news: This means that most of the features in projects like InstaPython and InstaPython-App as well as the incorporated projects like InstaBot won't work anymore. Since I am not really motivated nor have the energy to find alternatives and rewrite the programs, I am discontinuing these projects. That sadly means that you won't be able to use them anymore.

I am sorry for the inconveniences!



PS: I will probably release a compiled collection of the things that still work though!
